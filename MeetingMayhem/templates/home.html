Scenario:

You are an agent embedded in hostile territory. One of your fellow agents has been caught, and your mission is to aid them in their escape. You are attempting to arrange a meeting with other allied agents to exchange information critical to this mission. However, your only means of communication with your team has been compromised by a hostile Adversary. Can you successfully arrange a safe meeting with your team, or will the Adversary trick you into meeting somewhere unsafe?



Directions:

  • Send messages to your allies to arrange a meeting time and place.
  • The majority of the players need to vote for the same safe location to win.
  • The Adversary will also suggest a location, and that location will not be safe.
  • Beware of the Adversary attempting to cause confusion or trick you.
  • The Adversary is able to create, edit, and delete messages.
  • When available, use cryptography primitives to your advantage.
  • Encryption: encrypt a message with a recipient's public key.
  • Successful encryption will prevent the Adversary from readying your message.
  • Signing: sign a message with your private key.
  • A successful signature will validate that the message came from you.
  • You can also include multiple cryptography primitives in one message.
  • This can be useful if you are sending a message to multiple people.
  • When you are ready to vote for a meeting time and place, click the "Ready to Vote" button.
